Coronaviruses

Diseases

Coronaviruses are from the family of RNA viruses , and they cause illness in mammals, including humans and birds. The four significant variations of these viruses are Alpha, Beta, Gamma and Delta. Infections caused by these viruses can be mild and fatal, and in particular, these viruses cause respiratory tract infections in humans and birds. Symptoms of mild illnesses can include the common cold in humans, and fatal variations can cause MERS, Covid-19 and SARS.

A variant that has now become widely recognized of the coronaviruses's is the beta variant, the type that causes the Covid-19 in humans.

In total, there are six different variant strains of these coronaviruses which can infect humans, and some of them include the following:

  • SARS-CoV-2, the type which is the cause of the Covid-19 pandemic

  • MERS-CoV

  • SARS-CoV

History

The first case was recorded in the late 1920s, when a severe infection of respiratory infection in domesticated chickens was indentified in North America. Coronaviruses which infect humans were discovered in the 1960s, with the help of two methods.

While working in the Common Cold Unit of the British Medical Research Council, E.C. Kendall, Malcolm Bynoe, and David Tyrrel collected a unique common cold - B814. An attempt was made to grow the cold virus using techniques that had been succesfull in growing other viruses such as Rhinoviruses, other common cold viruses and adenoviruses, but the attempt failed. However, a new method was introduced by Dr. Bertil Hoorn. While experimenting, when an isolated sample of the B814 virus was placed in the noses of volunteers, it caused a cold in the volunteers.

As time went on many other human coronaviruses have been identified such as the SARS-CoV in 2003, HCoV NL63 in 2003, HCoV HKU1 in 2004, MERS-CoV in 2013, and SARS-CoV-2 in 2019, amongst the human coronaviruses, a large number of animal coronaviruses have also been identified since the 1960s.

Structure

Coronaviruses get their name from its structure. The name 'Cornonavirus' come from the Lation word 'corona', which translates to 'crown'. And this referes back to the structure of the virus, because when the virus is examined under the electron microscope, the virus appears to have a fringe of large, bulbous surface projections looking like a crown.

Coronaviruses are a large, spherical particles with unique surface projections. The size of a coronavirus is variable, averaging between 80 to 120 nms. And the total molecular weight on average is 40,000 kDa. Coronaviruses are enclosed in an enveloped, studded with projecting protein moleclues. These different layer of the coronaviruses help to protect the Coronavirus when it is on outside of the host cell.

The envelope is made up of a lipid bilayer, in which membrane (M), envelope (E) and spike (S) structural proteins are anchored. The E and M are the structural proteins which are combined with the lipid layer to shape the viral envelope and maintain its size. However, the S protein is required for interactions with the host cell.

Symptoms

People with Covid-19 may experience the following symptoms:

  • Fever or chills
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Headache
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Diarrhea
